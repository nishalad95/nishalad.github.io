ML Projects & Articles

Can Twitter Predict the Peak of a Pandemic?

Nature Scientific Report September 2021: During the early stages of the COVID-19 pandemic, understanding the evolution of virus transmission before mass testing was made readily available is important for policy makers & government officials. This work involved analysing Twitter data to extract useful insights during the first wave of the pandemic in England. Techniques involved were statistical, NLP & ML algorithms to help predict when an outbreak might occur as well as the attitudes in the population regarding specific government measures.

The ATLAS Detector

The Large Hadron Collider houses the ATLAS detector; weighing 7000 tonnes and located 100m below ground level. ATLAS is designed to observe up to 1.7 billion proton-proton collisions per second, with over 100 million electronic readout channels and a combined data volume of more than 60 million MB/s.
